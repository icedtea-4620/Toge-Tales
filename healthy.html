HEALTHY LUMPIANG

TOGE

BEHIND THE

FLAVOR

Healthy Lumpiang Toge is a lighter take on the classic Filipino street-food favorite, designed to keep the signature crunch while reducing excess oil. Instead of deep frying, it focuses on balance and freshness. The flavor comes from naturally sweet vegetables, lightly seasoned with garlic, onion, and pepper, then cooked just enough to highlight their clean, savory taste.



“The green-powered contender — Healthy Lumpiang Toge! Crispy without the guilt, veggie-loaded inside, unleashing a clean crunch combo that fuels your next move.”



INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups toge (mung bean sprouts)
  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • Spring roll wrappers
  • Oil for frying (minimal)

PROCEDURE

1. Sauté garlic, onion, and siling labuyo until fragrant.

2. Add the carrots and cook for 1–2 minutes.

3. Add the toge, soy sauce, salt, and pepper.

Mix and cook briefly.

4. Turn off heat and let the filling cool.

5. Spoon the mixture onto wrappers and roll into lumpia.

6. Heat a little oil and fry until golden and crispy

(or bake/air-fry for a lighter version).

7. erve warm, optionally with a light vinegar or calamansi dip.
