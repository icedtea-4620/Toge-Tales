SPICY LUMPIANG

TOGE

BEHIND THE

FLAVOR

Spicy Lumpiang Toge is a modern twist on the classic Filipino spring roll, inspired by the original Chinese-style lumpia introduced to the Philippines. As Filipinos adapted the dish, chili and bold spices were added to give it a hotter, more exciting flavor. This fusion of Chinese roots and Filipino love for spicy food created a crunchy, fiery version that’s now a favorite street-food upgrade.



“The fiery challenger of street-food battles — Spicy Lumpiang Toge! Crispy armor outside, chili-charged veggie power inside, unleashing a bold crunch attack with every bite.”



INGREDIENTS

  • 2 cups toge (mung bean sprouts)
  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 1 small onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 pcs siling labuyo, chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 tbsp chili garlic oil
  • Oil for frying
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • Salt & pepper to taste
  • Spring roll wrappers
  • Oil for frying

PROCEDURE

1. Sauté garlic, onion, and siling labuyo until fragrant.

2. Add the carrots and cook for 1–2 minutes.

3. Add the toge, soy sauce, chili garlic oil, salt, and pepper.

Mix and cook briefly.

4. Turn off heat and let the filling cool.

5. Spoon the mixture onto wrappers and roll into lumpia.

6. Heat oil and fry until golden and crispy.

7. Serve with spicy vinegar dipping sauce.
